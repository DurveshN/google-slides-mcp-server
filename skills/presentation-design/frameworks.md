# Presentation Design — Frameworks Reference

## Pitch Deck Framework

**Audience**: Investors, VCs, angels
**Goal**: Raise funding
**Duration**: 10-20 minutes | **Slides**: 8-15
**Key Principle**: Investors spend ~3 min per deck. Every word must earn its place.

### Slide Structure

| # | Slide | Purpose | Action Title Example |
|---|-------|---------|---------------------|
| 1 | **Title + Hook** | Company name + value prop | "AI-Powered Supply Chain Platform Reducing Costs 40%" |
| 2 | **Problem** | Visceral pain with data | "Manufacturers Lose $2B/Year to Inventory Inefficiency" |
| 3 | **Solution** | How you solve it | "Predictive AI Eliminates Stockouts Before They Happen" |
| 4 | **Why Now** | Market timing | "IoT + Cloud Compute Make Real-Time Inventory Possible" |
| 5 | **Market Size** | TAM/SAM/SOM | "$12B TAM Growing 25% YoY" |
| 6 | **Product/Demo** | How it works | "3-Click Setup, Real-Time Dashboard, Predictive Alerts" |
| 7 | **Business Model** | Revenue + pricing | "$500/Month, 120% Net Revenue Retention" |
| 8 | **Traction** | Actual metrics | "$150K ARR, 40 Customers, 15% MoM Growth" |
| 9 | **Competition** | Differentiation | "Only Platform Combining Predictive AI with Vendor Integration" |
| 10 | **Team** | Why this team wins | "Ex-Amazon Supply Chain + ex-Palantir ML" |
| 11 | **Financials** | 3-5 year projections | "Path to $10M ARR in 3 Years" |
| 12 | **The Ask** | Specific funding request | "$2M Series A to Scale to 200 Customers" |

### Visual Templates by Slide

| Slide | Recommended Visual | Fallback |
|-------|-------------------|----------|
| Title + Hook | Full-bleed cover image or big number | Clean text with accent shape |
| Problem | Before/after split or pain-point illustration | Icon grid of pain points |
| Solution | Architecture diagram or process flowchart | Screenshot/mockup with annotations |
| Why Now | Timeline showing market evolution | Metric cards with trend arrows |
| Market Size | Big number + supporting chart | TAM/SAM/SOM pyramid diagram |
| Product/Demo | Screenshot carousel or mockup grid | Feature icon grid (3-5 items) |
| Business Model | Simple diagram or pricing cards | Clean text with metric highlight |
| Traction | Metric dashboard (2-3 big numbers) | Growth chart with annotations |
| Competition | Comparison table or quadrant chart | Differentiator icon grid |
| Team | Photo grid or org chart | Text with accent shapes |
| Financials | Line chart (projection) or bar chart | Metric cards with milestones |
| The Ask | Clean text with strong CTA shape | Summary icon grid |

**Visual Strategy**: Lead with data visualizations and diagrams. Investors process charts faster than text. Use generated images for conceptual slides (Problem, Solution, Why Now). Use screenshots for Product. Use charts for Market, Traction, Financials.

### Quality Checks
- Traction numbers are ACTUALS, not projections
- Market size shows TAM/SAM/SOM calculation
- Ask is specific: "$X for Y over Z months"
- Problem is visceral with real data
- Total slides ≤15

### Palettes
- Corporate Navy (#1B2A4A / #FFFFFF / #4A90D9)
- Tech Startup (#0A192F / #F8F9FA / #64FFDA)
- Consulting (#FFFFFF / #F5F5F5 / #003366)

## Sales Deck Framework

**Audience**: Customers, prospects
**Goal**: Sell product/service
**Duration**: 15-30 minutes | **Slides**: 10-20
**Key Principle**: Every slide answers "What's in it for me?"

### Slide Structure

| # | Slide | Purpose | Action Title Example |
|---|-------|---------|---------------------|
| 1 | **Hook** | Grab attention | "60% of Sales Teams Miss Quota Due to Poor Lead Qualification" |
| 2 | **Problem** | Prospect's pain | "Your Team Spends 40% of Time on Unqualified Prospects" |
| 3 | **Agitate** | Cost of inaction | "That's $500K in Lost Pipeline Per Quarter" |
| 4 | **Solution** | How you fix it | "AI Lead Scoring Predicts Deal Close with 85% Accuracy" |
| 5 | **How It Works** | Simple process | "Connect CRM → Score Leads → Prioritize Outreach" |
| 6 | **Features → Benefits** | Map to outcomes | "Automated Scoring Means Reps Focus on Deals That Close" |
| 7 | **Social Proof** | Case studies | "How Company X Increased Close Rate 35% in 90 Days" |
| 8 | **ROI Calculator** | Show the math | "Save 20 hours/week = $50K/quarter in Productivity" |
| 9 | **Pricing** | Clear tiers | "Starter: $99/mo | Growth: $299/mo | Enterprise: Custom" |
| 10 | **FAQ/Objections** | Address concerns | "Implementation in 48 Hours, SOC2 Compliant" |
| 11 | **Next Steps** | Specific CTA | "Start Your Free Trial — See First Scores in 24 Hours" |

### Visual Templates by Slide

| Slide | Recommended Visual | Fallback |
|-------|-------------------|----------|
| Hook | Big number or striking statistic | Bold statement with accent shape |
| Problem | Before/after comparison or pain illustration | Icon grid of pain points |
| Agitate | Cost calculator diagram or metric cards | Bold text with warning accent |
| Solution | Product screenshot or demo GIF | Feature icon grid |
| How It Works | 3-step flowchart or process diagram | Numbered icon list |
| Features → Benefits | Icon grid with outcome labels | Two-column feature/benefit table |
| Social Proof | Quote card with company logo | Result metric with context |
| ROI Calculator | Interactive chart or savings timeline | Simple formula diagram |
| Pricing | Pricing cards with tier visuals | Clean comparison table |
| FAQ/Objections | Accordion-style or icon-per-answer | Clean bullet list (OK here) |
| Next Steps | CTA button graphic or roadmap | Clean text with arrow shape |

**Visual Strategy**: Show the transformation. Use before/after visuals heavily. Make ROI tangible with calculators and charts. Product screenshots are essential — prospects need to see the interface.

### Quality Checks
- More "you" than "we" (aim for 3:1 ratio)
- Specific ROI numbers, not vague claims
- Social proof matches target industry
- Clear next step with low friction
- Every feature maps to a benefit

### Palettes
- Tech Startup (#0A192F / #F8F9FA / #64FFDA)
- Creative (#FAFAFA / #F0F0F0 / #FF6B6B)
- Consulting (#FFFFFF / #F5F5F5 / #003366)

## Training Deck Framework

**Audience**: Students, employees
**Goal**: Teach or train
**Duration**: 30-60 minutes | **Slides**: 20-40
**Key Principle**: One concept per module. Never introduce two new ideas on one slide.

### Slide Structure

| # | Slide | Purpose | Action Title Example |
|---|-------|---------|---------------------|
| 1 | **Title + Objectives** | What they'll learn | "By the End: Build SQL Queries, Optimize Performance" |
| 2 | **Agenda** | Module breakdown | "3 Modules: Basics (20min) → Joins (25min) → Optimization (15min)" |
| 3 | **Why This Matters** | Relevance | "Poor Queries Cost Our Team 10 Hours/Week" |
| 4 | **Module 1: Concept** (5-8 slides) | Teach step by step | "Module 1: Writing Your First Query" |
| 5 | **Module 1: Example** (2-3 slides) | Real scenario | "Customer Support Needs Orders from Last 30 Days" |
| 6 | **Module 1: Practice** (2-3 slides) | Exercise | "Write a Query to Find Top 10 Customers by Revenue" |
| 7-9 | **Modules 2-4** | Repeat pattern | [Same structure] |
| 10 | **Summary** | Key takeaways | "Remember: Index Early, Filter Before Join" |
| 11 | **Assessment** | Test application | "Given This Slow Query, What 3 Things Would You Check?" |
| 12 | **Next Steps** | Apply learning | "Optimize One Query This Week, Share Results in Slack" |

### Visual Templates by Slide

| Slide | Recommended Visual | Fallback |
|-------|-------------------|----------|
| Title + Objectives | Learning path illustration or icon grid | Clean text with objectives list |
| Agenda | Timeline or module roadmap | Numbered list with icons |
| Why This Matters | Impact illustration or cost diagram | Metric cards showing savings |
| Concept | Diagram, architecture, or annotated screenshot | Step-by-step flowchart |
| Example | Screenshot with annotations or scenario illustration | Code snippet with callouts |
| Practice | Exercise card or interactive mockup | Clear instructions with icon |
| Summary | Mind map or key concept diagram | Icon grid of takeaways |
| Assessment | Quiz card or scenario illustration | Question with multiple choices |
| Next Steps | Action roadmap or checklist graphic | Clean CTA with arrow |

**Visual Strategy**: Training decks need to reduce cognitive load. Use diagrams to explain concepts, screenshots for examples, and interactive-looking cards for practice. Icon grids help learners mentally organize information. Avoid dense text — learners need to focus on the instructor.

### Quality Checks
- Each module: Concept → Example → Practice
- Learning objectives use Bloom's action verbs
- Titles are action-oriented ("Build a Query" not "Queries")
- Assessment tests application, not memorization
- Font size ≥24pt for body text
- One new concept per slide

### Palettes
- Healthcare (#FFFFFF / #E8F4F8 / #2E8B8B)
- ESG Green (#FFFFFF / #E8F5E9 / #2E7D32)
- Minimalist (#FFFFFF / #F8F9FA / #212529)

## Conference Deck Framework

**Audience**: Conference attendees
**Goal**: Inform, persuade, inspire
**Duration**: 15-30 minutes | **Slides**: 10-20
**Key Principle**: Slides are illustrations, not notes. The speaker carries the content.

### Slide Structure

| # | Slide | Purpose | Action Title Example |
|---|-------|---------|---------------------|
| 1 | **Hook** | Grab in 10 seconds | "Every Day, Humans Generate 2.5 Quintillion Bytes" |
| 2 | **Personal Story** | Why YOU care | "Three Years Ago, I Lost 200 Hours to Bad Data" |
| 3 | **Problem/Idea** | Core concept | "Data Teams Spend 80% of Time Cleaning, Not Analyzing" |
| 4 | **Evidence** (2-4 slides) | Research, data | "Companies with Clean Data Pipelines Grow 3x Faster" |
| 5 | **Turning Point** | "Aha" moment | "What If We Automated the 80%?" |
| 6 | **Implications** | Why it matters | "Analysts Become Strategic Advisors, Not Spreadsheet Jockeys" |
| 7 | **Call to Action** | What they should do | "Start With One Automated Pipeline This Month" |
| 8 | **Memorable Close** | Callback to hook | "Turn Data Chaos Into Clarity — Starting Tomorrow" |

### Visual Templates by Slide

| Slide | Recommended Visual | Fallback |
|-------|-------------------|----------|
| Hook | Full-bleed striking image or giant statistic | Bold statement on solid color |
| Personal Story | Photo of speaker or relevant scene | Quote card with attribution |
| Problem/Idea | Illustration showing the problem visually | Icon grid representing pain points |
| Evidence | Chart or infographic with key finding | Big number with supporting context |
| Turning Point | Split-screen before/after or metaphor image | Bold statement with visual accent |
| Implications | Future-state illustration or diagram | Outcome icon grid |
| Call to Action | Roadmap or step-by-step visual | Clean CTA with directional arrow |
| Memorable Close | Full-bleed image echoing opening | Callback quote with visual anchor |

**Visual Strategy**: Conference decks should work without the speaker. Use full-bleed images, large typography, and minimal text. Every slide is a visual hook. Generated images are perfect for metaphors and emotional moments. Data slides need bold, simple charts.

### Quality Checks
- Slides work WITHOUT the speaker (shareable)
- One dominant visual per slide
- Text <10 words per slide where possible
- Opening and closing connect (full circle)
- Emotional arc: tension → turning point → resolution
- No bullet points (visuals, quotes, single statements)

### Palettes
- Bold (#000000 / #FFFFFF / #FFD700)
- Dark Mode (#121212 / #1E1E1E / #BB86FC)
- Creative (#FAFAFA / #F0F0F0 / #FF6B6B)

## Data Deck Framework

**Audience**: Executives, board, analysts
**Goal**: Share data, insights, recommendations
**Duration**: 20-45 minutes | **Slides**: 10-25
**Key Principle**: Every chart answers one specific question.

### Slide Structure

| # | Slide | Purpose | Action Title Example |
|---|-------|---------|---------------------|
| 1 | **Title + KPIs** | Key metrics at a glance | "Q4 2025: Revenue Up 23%, Churn Down 15%, NPS 72" |
| 2 | **Context** | Time period, scope | "Data Covers NA/EU, Oct 1 — Dec 31, 2025" |
| 3 | **Key Finding 1** (2-3 slides) | Metric → Chart → Insight | "Revenue Grew from $2.1M to $2.6M QoQ" |
| 4 | **Key Finding 2** (2-3 slides) | Repeat pattern | "Churn Decreased 15% Due to Onboarding Improvements" |
| 5 | **Key Finding 3** (2-3 slides) | Repeat pattern | "CAC Increased 40% — Paid Search Costs Rising" |
| 6 | **Trends** (2-3 slides) | Long-term patterns | "ARR Shows Consistent 20%+ YoY Growth" |
| 7 | **Segmentation** (2-3 slides) | Breakdown by dimension | "Enterprise Generates 5x More Revenue Than SMB" |
| 8 | **Anomalies** (1-2 slides) | What stood out | "February Dip: API Outage Caused 3-Day Usage Drop" |
| 9 | **Recommendations** (2-3 slides) | Data-driven actions | "Double Enterprise Sales Team (Projected +$500K Q1)" |
| 10 | **Appendix** (3-5 slides) | Detailed data | [Complete dashboards, methodology] |

### Visual Templates by Slide

| Slide | Recommended Visual | Fallback |
|-------|-------------------|----------|
| Title + KPIs | Metric cards dashboard (2-4 big numbers) | Clean list with accent numbers |
| Context | Map or scope diagram | Simple text with date range |
| Key Finding | Chart (bar/line) + annotation callouts | Big number + supporting text |
| Trends | Line chart with trend line | Sparkline grid |
| Segmentation | Stacked bar or pie chart | Comparison table with visuals |
| Anomalies | Annotated timeline or highlighted chart | Before/after comparison |
| Recommendations | ROI projection chart or impact diagram | Numbered recommendation cards |
| Appendix | Detailed tables and supplementary charts | Clean data tables |

**Visual Strategy**: Data decks live and die by chart quality. Every chart must answer one question. Use annotations to highlight insights, not just describe data. Big number slides break up chart density. Use consistent colors for the same metrics across slides. Generated images can illustrate recommendations and impact scenarios.

### Quality Checks
- Chart type matches data type
- Axes labeled with units and source cited
- Annotations highlight insights (not just describe)
- Appendix exists for detailed data
- No 3D effects or chart junk
- Each chart answers ONE specific question

### Palettes
- Corporate Navy (#1B2A4A / #FFFFFF / #4A90D9)
- Finance (#1E1E1E / #F5F5F5 / #00C853)
- Minimalist (#FFFFFF / #F8F9FA / #212529)

### Chart Selection Guide

| Data Type | Best Chart | Why |
|-----------|-----------|-----|
| Trend over time | Line chart | Shows continuous change |
| Comparing categories | Bar chart | Easy to rank |
| Parts of a whole | Pie/donut (max 5 items) | Simple proportions |
| Correlation | Scatter plot | Shows relationships |
| Distribution | Histogram/box plot | Shows spread |

**Rules**: Pie max 5 slices, no 3D. Bar starts at 0. Line max 5 lines. All labels + units + source.
